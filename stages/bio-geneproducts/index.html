Biology 101 - Gene Products

Background

RNA and Proteins are called "Gene Products" because they are coded for by genes in the DNA. RNA is produced in a process called transcription. Transcription requires a DNA template, which we call a gene. Proteins are produced through translation. Translation requires an RNA template.

Proteins and RNA often help chemical reactions to proceed. In a chemical reaction, reagents are converted to products. When chemical reactions occur in cells, we call the reagents and the products "Metabolites." Metabolites are often molecules like lipids, sugars, amino acids. Metabolites are not coded for by genes.
