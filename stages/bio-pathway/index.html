Biology 101 - Metabolic Pathway

Background

A metabolic pathway is a series of metabolic reactions chained together. Examples of metabolic pathways include the conversions of glucose into energy and cholesterol into steroid hormones.

The image below is a first introduction to depicting pathways using our WikiPathways format: Metabolites are blue and Gene Products are black. The conversion from one metabolite to another is indicated by a line with a solid arrowhead pointing to the reaction product. The protein enzymes that catalyze each reaction are connected to the conversion lines by a catalysis line that ends with an open circle.
