Draw a Conversion

Background

The conversion of one metabolite into another comprises the fundamental biological process of metabolism. Often catalyzed by enzymes and chained together in a series, each conversion reaction involves a change in the chemical structure of a metabolite or small molecule. Conversion reactions are thus drawn as two metabolites and a mim-conversion interaction, pointing from the subtrate to the product.

Your Mission

Model the conversion reaction depicted in this research article figure.



Ferraldeschi et al., Targeting the androgen receptor pathway in castration-resistant prostate cancer: progresses and prospects. Oncogene. 2015 Apr.

  • Launch PathVisio. To open the starter pathway, go to Plugins > WikiPathways > Search. Type in "WP3900" in the Keyword Search tab and click Enter. When the search results load, double-click the entry for WP3900 to open it.
  • Perform the challenge tasks...
    • Select Met from the Data Node palette in the Objects tab
    • Click on canvas to place the metabolite node
    • Double-click on node to open its properties
    • Search for “glucose”; Choose any of the entries that just say “glucose”; Click OK
    • Repeat these steps to make “glucose-6-phosphate” metabolite node
    • Select the mim-conversion arrow from the MIM interactions palette in the Objects tab
    • Drag and drop anywhere on canvas to create the interaction
    • Drag each end of the interaction over a target area on each node; Note source vs target
    • (Optional) Align nodes using toolbar actions
    • Done!
  • When you have completed the challenge, save your work as a GPML file under File > Save As.
  • Drag-and-drop the GPML file below to submit.
